🚀 3D Printed Pedal-Operated Trash Bin – Design & Engineering Showcase

📌 Project Overview

This 3D-printed prototype of a pedal-operated trash bin integrates mechanical design, motion study, and additive manufacturing to demonstrate an efficient and ergonomic waste management solution. The design ensures hands-free operation, optimizing both hygiene and usability.

🛠️ Skills Demonstrated

  • ✔ Mechanical Design – Engineered for smooth pedal operation, linkage mechanisms, and structural stability.
  • ✔ 3D CAD Modeling – Designed in Fusion 360, ensuring precision, manufacturability, and optimized assembly.
  • ✔ Additive Manufacturing (3D Printing) – Utilized FDM printing to prototype a functional mechanical system.
  • ✔ Material Selection – PLA for prototyping; ABS, PETG, or metal-reinforced plastic for real-world applications.
  • ✔ Motion Study & Kinematic Analysis – Simulated in Fusion 360 to validate pedal movement, force distribution, and component interaction.
  • ✔ Design for Manufacturing (DFM) & Assembly (DFA) – Simplified part integration for efficient production and user-friendly assembly.

⚙️ Motion Analysis & Mechanism Design

  • Linkage System: The pedal operates via a lever-arm mechanism, smoothly lifting the lid with minimal force.
  • Load Distribution: The foot pedal, hinges, and lid movement were analyzed to ensure long-term durability.
  • Friction & Wear Considerations: Parts were optimized to reduce mechanical wear and improve the lifespan of the product.
  • Tested Opening Angle: Ensures maximum lid clearance while keeping the center of gravity stable.

💡 Challenges & Learnings

🔴 Challenges Faced

  • ❌ Balancing Aesthetics & Functionality – The challenge was to keep the design visually appealing while ensuring structural strength.
  • ❌ Ensuring Smooth Pedal Action – Required precise hinge alignment and lever optimization to achieve the right force balance.
  • ❌ Material Limitations in 3D Printing – PLA and ABS have different flexural strengths, so reinforcement strategies were considered.

✅ Key Learnings

  • ✔ Iterative Prototyping – Multiple design refinements improved mechanism efficiency and part strength.
  • ✔ Material Optimization – Layer orientation and print settings significantly affect component strength and durability.
  • ✔ DFM for Mass Production – The 3D-printed version helped identify potential molded plastic or metal alternatives for large-scale manufacturing.

🌱 Engineering Ethics & Sustainable Design

  • ♻ Eco-Friendly Approach – Designed with biodegradable PLA & recyclable materials to reduce environmental impact.
  • 🛠️ Optimized for Manufacturing – Simplified part count and assembly process for ease of production.
  • 👥 User-Centric Design – Focused on ergonomics, accessibility, and hygiene benefits for daily use.

📌 Prototype Specifications

  • Technology Used: FDM 3D Printing
  • Material: PLA (Prototype), ABS/PETG (Future Versions)
  • Mechanism: Foot-Pedal Actuated Lid System
  • Additional Features: Removable Inner Bin, Anti-Slip Base, Stainless Steel Handle
